Berlin

Eleven hotels across Mitte, Kreuzberg, Friedrichshain, and Schoneberg. Each one shaped by the neighbourhood it lives in.

Berlin does not do quiet mornings. Neither does AMANO. The city invented the hotel bar, the rooftop session, the dinner that turns into dancing. AMANO was born here. Not as a chain, but as a series of places that belong where they are.

Neighbourhoods

Start with the street.
The hotel follows.

Mitte

The centre. Culture, commerce, and the bars that tie them together.

Kreuzberg

The edge. Markets, canals, and the kind of energy Berlin is actually known for.

Friedrichshain

Wall-side. River-facing. Still louder than everywhere else.

Schoneberg

Old Berlin. Named for Romy Schneider. Quieter, warmer, and full of surprises.
